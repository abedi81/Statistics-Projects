# Statistics Projects

## Overview

This repository focuses on building a deep understanding of statistical concepts through simulation, modeling, and practical analysis.

Rather than relying only on theoretical formulas, the projects emphasize translating statistical ideas into computational experiments to develop intuition about uncertainty, estimation, and data-generating processes.

The goal is to bridge the gap between statistical theory and real-world application.

---

## Core Themes

The projects in this repository explore key areas of statistics:

- Probabilistic reasoning and Bayesian inference  
- Simulation-based methods (Monte Carlo)  
- Statistical properties of estimators  
- Regression and econometric fundamentals  
- Time-to-event and survival analysis  
- Convergence and sampling behavior  

---

## Current Projects

### 1. Bayesian Monte Carlo Simulation

Simulation-based approach to Bayesian inference in a medical testing setting.

- Demonstrates how posterior probabilities emerge from repeated sampling  
- Compares simulated results with analytical solutions  
- Shows convergence behavior and uncertainty reduction  

---

### 2. Error vs Residual in Regression

A simulation-based exploration of fundamental OLS concepts.

- Distinguishes true errors from estimated residuals  
- Demonstrates OLS properties (orthogonality, zero-sum conditions)  
- Explains unbiasedness and sampling variability  

---

### 3. Survival Analysis (Weibull)

Parametric survival analysis using simulated time-to-event data.

- Models failure behavior with censoring  
- Estimates survival and hazard functions  
- Computes reliability metrics (MTTF, B-life)  
- Validates model fit using statistical tests  

---

## Approach

Across all projects, the emphasis is on:

- Building intuition through simulation  
- Understanding assumptions and limitations  
- Connecting theory to observable data behavior  
- Interpreting results in a practical context
